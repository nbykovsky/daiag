Beneath the dawn, the river gathers light.
Each stone remembers rain in silver hush.
A heron lifts the morning with one wing.
The fields awaken under threads of gold.
Wind carries seeds through corridors of grass.
An old path bends toward roofs of distant smoke.
The sky holds every promise without sound.
My footsteps learn the patience of the earth.
Evening will fold the hills in violet shade.
Stars will arrive like lanterns after rain.
Moonlight will trace the river's quiet name.
The reeds will answer softly from the shore.
Night will settle gently on the listening fields.
